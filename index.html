Overview: History and Modern Trends in Version Control Systems

Version Control Systems (VCS) are tools that track changes in files, especially source code. They allow teams to collaborate, record a history of modifications, and revert to previous versions when needed.

What Are Version Control Systems?

VCS help developers work together efficiently, whether on local machines or through cloud platforms. They can be centralized (with one main repository) or distributed (each developer has a full copy of the project).

Why It Matters

  • Maintains a full history of project changes.
  • Enables collaboration among multiple contributors.
  • Supports branching, merging, and experimentation safely.
  • Forms the backbone of modern DevOps and automation pipelines.
